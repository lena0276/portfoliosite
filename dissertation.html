Welcome to Folk Theories of Corporate Social Responsibility website!

This website provides access to project details, data, and reflections from ‘Folk Theories: Corporate Social Responsibility and Philanthropy in our Digital Society’ project. The intent behind both the research and this project site is to bring awareness to how corporations preform their social responsibilities and philanthropic endeavors in our digital society. While seeking to understand how everyday individuals recognize, acknowledge, and grasp the social responsibilities and corresponding actions of corporations.

Reflection: How I came to study Folk Theories of CSR/P in our Digital Society?

‘Folk Theories of CSR/P in our Digital Society’ is the culmination of two years of academic inquiry around how United States cultural institutions could successfully engage visitors through the implementation of immersive technology. Like most research projects, questions often lead to more questions prompting the research to take on new approaches, take steps back, change directions, and so on. This research project is the outcome of just that – a question that turned into a project, leading to a master’s program, where learning informed a change of direction, uncovering an unknown interest, and thus resulting in a dissertation project on CSR/P. When I began research CP in the fall of my master’s program, my hope was I would be able to find a solution to needs of digital resources, specialized expertise, and funding cultural institutions need to bring technology to their visitor experiences, however, I discovered an area of academic inquiry that was simultaneously vast and limited in the specific information I was looking for. Additionally, I ran into a tiny little roadblock called: I knew nothing about what CSR/P was and how it operated in our Digital Society. So, I decided, if I eventually wanted to find a corporate solution to the cultural sector’s needs, I first needed to understand the behind the scenes of CSR/P.

Information of Ethical Approval

This research project is conducted as an Independent Student Portfolio with the objective of fulfilling the master’s dissertation requirement for University of Glasgow’s MSc. Digital Society program. The entirety of the research project has been approved by the University of Glasgow’s ethics review board. And in accordance with the ethical review process all data published to the project site has been anonymized for the protection of participants identities. All research is conducted by the primary researcher Lena Weisman and overseen by an academic advisor, Dr. Elisabetta Ferrari.
